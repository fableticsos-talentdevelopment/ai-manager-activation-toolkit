Manager Guides for AI-Enabled Leadership

Managers make AI real. These guides help them lead.

AI is changing how work gets done across every function of the business. New tools appear constantly, but technology alone does not change how an organization works. Managers do.

Every investment Fabletics makes in AI becomes meaningful when managers help their teams work differently. They identify opportunities, redesign work, build new habits, create space for experimentation, and recognize progress.

These Field Guides are performance support resources designed to help managers lead AI-enabled improvement in the moments that matter. Each guide focuses on one management challenge and provides practical coaching, principles, and actionable tools you can apply immediately.

You don't need to read every guide from beginning to end. Start with the management challenge you're facing today. Return to the guides whenever you need them.

Workflow
Mapping Guide

Find the best opportunities to improve how work gets done.

Core Idea

The best AI improvements begin with the work, not the tool. Managers often ask, "Where can we use AI?" A better question is "What part of this work creates the most friction and the least value for people?" Start there.

What part of this work creates the most friction and the least value for people?
When to Use This Guide
·Your team isn't sure where to begin with AI.
·You have many ideas but no clear priorities.
·A workflow feels slower, harder, or more inconsistent than it should.
·You're preparing for a Workflow Improvement Sprint.
Before You Start

The best first workflows are repeatable, predictable, important enough to matter, and small enough to improve within a few weeks.

Don't start with the biggest or most broken process. Early wins build confidence, demonstrate value, and teach the team how to improve the next workflow.

02
Chapter Two

Map the Work

The Workflow Mapping Process

Look for work that happens frequently, frustrates the team, creates delays, or is performed inconsistently. Avoid rare activities, constantly changing processes, or work already being redesigned. Choose something important enough that people notice the improvement, but small enough to complete within a few weeks.

Watch for If everyone complains about the same task, you've probably found a good candidate.

The goal isn't to create a perfect process map. Capture the major stages rather than every click or decision. Pay attention to where work waits, where information changes hands, where people leave the official process, where someone says "it depends," and where experienced employees work differently than new employees.

Watch for If your process map matches the SOP exactly, you probably haven't mapped the real workflow.

Use the 5 Types of Workflow Friction as your diagnostic lens. Don't stop at identifying friction—ask why it exists. Sometimes AI is the answer. Sometimes the workflow itself needs to change before AI adds value.

Watch for Avoid automating a bad process. Fix unnecessary steps, unclear roles, or poor decisions before layering on AI.

Instead of asking where AI fits, ask what job someone is doing that AI could help perform. Think in verbs: draft, summarize, organize, compare, extract, rewrite, brainstorm, analyze, review, explain, or challenge.

Watch for The best AI opportunities usually remove friction while allowing people to focus on higher-value work.

The first workflow you improve isn't about maximizing ROI—it's about building confidence. Improve one workflow, test it, learn from it, refine it, and then move to the next opportunity.

Watch for Success isn't finishing one improvement. Success is creating a team that knows how to keep improving.

Chapter Three

Choose the Improvement

Manager Insight

Protect the Purpose of the Work

Before introducing AI into a workflow, ask:

What is the real purpose of this work?

Use AI to strengthen that purpose—not replace it.

If the highest value comes from preparing information, organizing data, or reducing repetitive effort, AI can often create significant value.

If the highest value comes from human judgment, discussion, coaching, relationship-building, or decision-making, AI should support those activities—not replace them.

A common question

"Can AI do this?"

A better question

"Should AI do this?"

Use AI to remove friction from the work—not the value from the work.

Types of Work AI Can Help With

Think in verbs, not tools

Rather than focusing on AI tools, think about the job that needs to be done.

Common Traps
—Starting with the AI tool instead of the work.
—Trying to redesign everything at once.
—Mistaking busy work for high-value work.
—Optimizing a workflow that doesn't meaningfully improve outcomes.
Next Step

Run a Workflow Improvement Sprint.

Once you've identified a worthwhile workflow, run a Workflow Improvement Sprint with your team to redesign the work, test improvements, and capture reusable assets.
